adapter-payments – my notes
----------------------------

what I built and why:
the starter had OrderService talking directly to FastPayClient and SafeCashClient - two
SDKs with completely different method signatures. FastPay has payNow(custId, amount),
SafeCash has createPayment(amount, user) then confirm() on the result. messy. I added a
PaymentGateway interface with a single charge(customerId, amountCents) method. then made
FastPayAdapter and SafeCashAdapter - each class takes its SDK in the constructor and in
charge() just translates the call to whatever that SDK needs. App builds a HashMap with
"fastpay" and "safecash" as keys and passes it into OrderService. OrderService just does
gateways.get(provider).charge(...) - thats it. want to add a third provider later? write
one new adapter class, add one line to the map in App. OrderService code stays untouched.

patterns used:
- Adapter (bridging incompatible interfaces behind a common target)
- Constructor injection (map of gateways injected into OrderService)

challenges:
the SafeCash one took a sec - its two-step: createPayment() returns a SafeCashPayment
object and you have to call confirm() on that to get the transaction id. so inside
SafeCashAdapter.charge() I had to do both in sequence and return the result of confirm().
once I realised I just had to chain the two calls it was straightforward. also made sure
both adapters validate null inputs with Objects.requireNonNull as the hints mentioned.
